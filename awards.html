Grants & Awards

  • Dec 2022. Postdoctoral Fellow Advising Grant. National Program Office, National Research Council Canada.
    Postdoctoral fellow will carry out original research on innovative projects, with opportunities for career development (publications or industry interaction). These fellowships are 2 year career development positions, aimed at bringing highly qualified personnel into the unique NRC environment. Project proposals will be assessed by an internal committee against 1) originality and innovation; 2) significance and expected contributions to research; 3) clarity and scope of objectives; 4) benefits to the postdoctoral fellow and 5) Feasibility.
  • Sept 2018. Rising Star. Digital Technologies Research Centre, National Research Council Canada.
    For an individual who, during his/her formative years with DT, has contributed far beyond expectations and who exemplifies NRC Values.
    1. Demonstrates initiative and potential as a future leader in his/her field or area of expertise;
    2. Achieves outcomes that align with DT’s strategic objectives, surpass expectations, and generate sustainable benefits with long-lasting impact for Canadians.
  • Jun 2014. ACL Student Travel Award. The Association of Computational Linguistics.
    For being student helper for the local committee of ACL-2014 and oral presentation of the paper titled "XMEANT: Better semantic MT evaluation without reference translations" in ACL-2014 Baltimore, Maryland, US.
  • Oct 2012. Research Travel Grant (RTG12/13.EG013). Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.
    For oral presentation of the paper titled "Unsupervised vs. Supervised Weight Estimation for Semantic MT Evaluation Metrics" in SSST-6, Jeju Island, South Korea.
  • Sept 2011. Research Travel Grant (RTG11/12.EG012). Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.
    For oral and poster presentation of the paper titled "SMT vs. AI redux: How semantic frames evaluate MT more accurately" in IJCAI-11, Barcelona, Spain.
  • Jun 2011. ACL Student Travel Award. The Association of Computational Linguistics.
    For being student helper for the local committee of ACL-2011 and oral presentation of the papers titled "MEANT: inexpensive, high-accuracy, semi-automatic metric for evaluating translation utility based on semantic roles" in ACL-2011 and "Structured vs. Flat Semantic Role Representations for Machine Translation Evaluation" in SSST-5, Portland, Oregon, US.
  • Sept 2009 - Dec 2014. Postgraduate Studentship. Hong Kong University of Science and Technology.
